Topic

I want to do my family history, but where do I start?

Inspiration

Main Points

  1. Get signed up. Sign up for FamilySearch, Ancestry, FindMyPast, and MyHeritage. Sign up
  2. Get organized. Make a GoogleDoc that helps you keep track of your research.
  3. Make a goal. At the top of the GoogleDoc, write a personal goal for this seven-week goal.

Week 2

Topic

How do I find names to take to the temple?

Inspiration

Video: The Jess Family

Main Points

  1. Sources. A source is a record (such as a census record, birth certificate, etc.) that is attached to an ancestor in your family tree. Sources are key—they validate an ancestor's existence.
  2. Three-source rule of thumb. Make sure that each ancestor has at least three sources attached.
  3. Foundation. Make sure everything later than the 1800's is accurate before going further back.

Assignment

Attach a source to an ancestor (even if they already have some sources attached).

Week 3

Topic

How do I research my ancestors?

Inspiration

Nephi's family history experience (from 1 Nephi chapters 3 & 4)

Main Points

  1. Record hints. When FamilySearch identifies a potential match with a record, it appears in the "record hints" section of the ancestor's profile page.
  2. Descendancy view. The descendancy view shows at-a-glance lots of potential work that can be done, such as record hints and temple opportunities.

Handout

Download

Assignment

Prayerfully choose an ancestor from your family tree. Click on their name, then click "Tree" to view their tree, then change the view from "traditional" to "descendancy" in the upper-left corner. See if there are opportunities to add records as sources or to do temple work.
